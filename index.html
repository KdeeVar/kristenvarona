E-Commerce · Supplements
Plexus Worldwide
Product LeadershipProgram MgmtDeliveryAgile Forecasting

Led enterprise-wide platform modernization for a direct sales e-commerce company — absorbed a mid-project Japan market expansion, built the backlog from zero, and shipped the full program in 14 months.

The Problem

Plexus Worldwide was mid-stream on an enterprise-wide platform modernization with multiple active engineering workstreams. My team owned the new infrastructure and delivery everything else depended on — and had an empty backlog when I arrived. Existing systems had to stay operational, the business was eager for progress, and legacy platform enhancements were being requested in parallel. Six months in, a Japan market expansion was added to the scope with a non-negotiable deadline.

What I Did
  • Worked with architecture to establish what had been built to date, the target future state, and the actual critical path
  • Built the backlog from scratch — gave a nine-person team of local, offshore, and contract engineers clear prioritized direction for the first time in the engagement
  • Blocked low value requests - setting appropriate priorities to ensure focus on the right items
  • Maintained a living roadmap, honest about what was coming, what was at risk, and why
  • Used agile forecasting for full transparency to show program management velocity/throughput, delivery timing, and dependency unblocking — real metrics, not status theater
  • When Japan market expansion was added mid-stream, absorbed the scope and re-sequenced priorities without collapsing the original timeline
  • Managed the ongoing prioritization tension between modernization work and legacy enhancement demands from a business with limited patience

Outcome: Full platform modernization shipped in 14 months. Japan market expansion delivered concurrently. The business had working software.

What This Demonstrates

The ability to establish clarity and drive delivery across a complex multi-team program — using lean practices as real management tools, not process overhead.

02
SaaS · Workflow · Co-founder · 0→1
Kanban Zone
SaaS0→1WorkflowTechnically Fluent

Co-founded and built a workflow productivity SaaS from zero — patented the core board architecture, owned the full product surface as CTO/COO and Product Manager, grew to 3,000+ users on a bootstrapped budget.

The Problem

Enterprise agile transformations hit a consistent wall: coaches could design the right workflow on a whiteboard, but no tool could replicate it digitally. Kanban boards were universally flat — one-dimensional grids that couldn't model how real work actually moves through an organization. Teams were forced to compromise their process to fit their tool.

What I Did
  • Designed and patented a flexible board engine — nested swimlanes, non-linear flows, multi-dimensional layouts — requiring a fundamental rethink of the underlying data model
  • Owned the full product surface (PDLC): customer discovery, feature prioritization, UX design, story writing, and acceptance criteria
  • Learned the MERN stack and AWS to close the gap between architectural decisions and engineering execution — and shipped code myself when speed mattered
  • Averaged multiple deploys per day using feature toggles, keeping bug response time under two hours
  • Diagnosed a conversion drop-off, redesigned and simplified the subscription model, and shipped the change
  • Declined to build a full project management feature set requested by a significant client — it would have compromised the product's identity as a lean workflow tool. The product stayed coherent.

Outcome: Grew to 3,000+ users — primarily teams of 30–50 members — five-star support rating throughout. Reached profitability on a bootstrapped budget. I exited to pursue other opportunities; the business continues to operate.

What This Demonstrates

The ability to hold CTO/COO, Product Manager, and founder responsibilities simultaneously — and to use technical depth as a product advantage, not a distraction. Including knowing when to say no to a paying customer.

03
Consumer SaaS · AI-Native
TrailOS / WildLog
Product Strategy0→1AI FeaturesConsumer SaaS

Defined and launched a three-product outdoor adventure SaaS suite — ran full product strategy before writing a line of code, shipped WildLog with AI-powered reporting as the live anchor product.

The Problem

Adventure memories have a fragmentation problem. Photos live in camera rolls with no context. Trip details disappear into social feeds with no personal reference. Gear accumulates with no system. Planning happens in notes apps and group chats. Nothing connects.

What I Did
  • Ran a full product process before writing code: market research, audience definition, competitive landscape analysis, product vision, and MVP scoping
  • Defined the TrailOS suite — WildLog (adventure journal), PackMind (gear management), TripForge (trip planning) — and sequenced WildLog first deliberately: without a log of past adventures, gear management and trip planning have no anchor
  • Built using Claude and a modern, maintainable web stack — prioritizing technology I could own long-term over novelty
  • Shipped AI capability directly to users: the Generate Report feature uses AI to transform unstructured adventure logs into coherent, shareable summaries
  • Launched as live SaaS with analytics and SEO instrumented from day one; social sharing emerging as primary organic growth driver
Outcome: TrailOS is a complete adventure system — Plan It, Live It, Log It. TripForge reduces decision fatigue by surfacing destination suggestions before you go. WildLog captures your basic notes on return and generates a story that seals the memory and makes it worth sharing. Both products are live, AI-enabled, and integrated as a seamless suite built and shipped solo in five weeks.
What This Demonstrates

The full product strategy arc — market validation, competitive analysis, audience definition, MVP scoping, and live launch — executed independently using current AI-native development practices.

04
Enterprise Retail · POS
Blue Yonder (previously JDA Software)
Retail TechEnterpriseDomain DepthTechnically Fluent

More than a decade building enterprise POS customizations for major North American and Latin American retailers — pricing engines, loyalty systems, multi-jurisdiction tax compliance, and a 7-hour → 7-minute performance overhaul.

The Problem

Large-format retailers needed complex, rules-based customizations the core platform couldn't deliver — kit pricing engines, loyalty integrations, multi-jurisdiction tax logic, and integrated payment systems. Zero-defect-tolerance environment: a pricing error means immediate, measurable revenue impact at every register.

Key Engagements
  • Cabela's — Kit Pricing Rules Engine. Designed and built a custom pricing rules engine for complex bundling logic: conditional item combinations, brand-specific eligibility, price-ceiling constraints, and substitution rules. Deterministic, auditable, performant at transaction speed.
  • Batch Processing Overhaul. Inherited a nightly inventory and pricing push to stores consuming seven hours. Diagnosed synchronous threading as root cause. Re-architected with multi-threading. Runtime: 7 hours → 7 minutes. Solo delivery on an unfamiliar stack.
  • Office Depot Latin America. Maintained codebases for three retail chains — Office Depot, RadioShack, and Marchand (Grupo Gigante) — across multiple Latin American countries. Managed competing priorities, packaged releases, and delivered country-specific fiscal reporting upgrades against hard compliance dates.

Outcome: Production systems serving millions of transactions. The batch optimization alone reclaimed six-plus hours of nightly processing time across an entire store network.

What This Demonstrates

Deep retail technology domain expertise — pricing, taxes, loyalty, payments, fiscal compliance — with the technical fluency to own both problem definition and solution.
